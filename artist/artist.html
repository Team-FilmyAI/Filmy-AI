About Me

My name is Emma Stone, and I’m an actress passionate about telling meaningful stories and bringing characters to life. Born on November 6, 1988, in Scottsdale, Arizona, I discovered my love for acting at a young age, performing in local theater productions before moving to Los Angeles to pursue my dream. My journey began with roles in films like Superbad (2007) and Easy A (2010), which marked a turning point in my career and earned me my first Golden Globe nomination. Since then, I’ve had the honor of working on a variety of projects, including The Help (2011), La La Land (2016)—where I was humbled to win an Academy Award—and The Favourite (2018), which allowed me to explore challenging and rewarding roles.

Acting has always been more than a profession for me—it’s a way to connect with people and share the complexities of human experiences. Whether it’s through the humor of a lighthearted comedy or the depth of a dramatic piece, I love immersing myself in stories that resonate with audiences. I’m also deeply committed to advocating for mental health awareness, a cause that’s close to my heart, and I strive to use my platform to inspire and create meaningful conversations.

Outside of my career, I value the importance of staying grounded and embracing life’s moments of joy and learning. From spending time with loved ones to supporting causes that align with my values, I’m constantly reminded of the importance of balancing work with what truly matters. As I continue on this journey, my goal is to keep growing as an artist and as a person, creating work that leaves a lasting impact and continues to bring people together.
